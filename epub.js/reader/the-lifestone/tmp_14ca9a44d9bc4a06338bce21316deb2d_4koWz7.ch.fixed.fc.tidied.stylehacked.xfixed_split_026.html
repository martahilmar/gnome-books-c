Chapter 21

There is one other person foretold of in the legends, one more critical to the completion of the reign of the dark one than even himself. I need to know whether the future is as it has been foretold, or has a catastrophe unfurled and is the future now placed in disruption and mire. I glance towards the Lifestone, knowing that a union with it once more is impossible to avoid, stepping towards it, trying to ignore the dark threatening ranting emanating from the cage of power; with a slight waft of my hand silence falls on the room. Nailstead is still ranting, but now the sound he is making only fills his temporary prison, his word and curses silenced to the outer world.

'Let him entertain himself' I thought, as I approached the nemesis and savior of the world I love. The crystal Lifestone waits for me, knowing that I will attend to it, now and for as long as breath fills my body, I am fated to attend to it though not to be controlled by it, I have decided.

The Lifestone welcomes my presence, so the thought must have been formed in my own mind and not presented by it to me. I reach out, placing my hands to either side of the ancient power source; not because I need contact to commune with it, but because I will need the support its physical presence offers to my body, as my mind departs both it and from within this cavern. The stone pulses just once and then my mind leaves my body behind my essence free floats separate but controlled.

I am high above the cavern, floating towards the distant field of battle, I can see forever or so it seems, though forever is an indefinable distance I tell myself. Time to work, to seek, to learn the truth of this our destiny, from high above I watch the last of the creatures passing through the valley. They marched in peace now, their thoughts fully fixed, their attention set only on obtaining their new home, the open abyss I have opened for them all. They are exactly as they should be, an enemy that is no more, a force that has been changed for the better. That is until the dark lord joins with them once more, then they will return to being what they always have been, but by then it will be to late for them all.

Below I can see the troops gathering around a single mounted figure, sitting astride and looking regal, mounted on a pure white horse, now being led towards the distant elfin castle by a dwarf. Him I know, next to him, carrying the black blade tightly wrapped in a thick cloth walks the Elf, Iffilditch. The weapon though wrapped still glows with the evil it contains. The King looks well, which is not as I expected or hoped it would be!

My mind sought the future; The King sitting inset on his throne, something he rarely chooses to do. All around him stand his followers, council members listening in rapt awe as he speaks of the peace to come. A lone rider races across the plain heading for the cavern I now called home, sent I sense to fetch the witch. He will be disappointed, access will not be granted to the rider and he will return in despair to his King, having failed in his duty.

Iffilditch kneels before the King carefully holding out the dark weapon. I notice something odd, a bandage, not a large swathing, but all the same a bandage. It is wrapped tightly around the King's wrist, passing between his thumb and finger's. Time flickers forward; The King stands raising his good hand demanding silence, a knight his head turned down kneels before the master. This is as it should be, the knight has returned to himself, the question in my mind is, has this knight completed his duty to fate, and legend, before being returned, or has he like the rider will, have failed?

For a second the babble continues before finally falling silent, the knight raises his head, the audience looked at their liege, all there witness the first thrust of fates given hand. They watch as the King, their King wobbles slightly, then his face contorts before them in a display of agony. He clutches at his bandage trying to rip it from his wrist, but the wrapping is too tight, and secured with metal pins, thus he also fails. The weapon of blackness pulses, it has been placed on a table and ignored I note, it resides on its table to the King's right, feeling the growth of its canker rising, flooding the King's blood with its poison, it duty done the blade waits.

Fate is as it had been decreed and a new evil has been created and released to torment the living. My mind found peace, all is not lost, the King is dying! Not swiftly but by inches, the poison now suffuses his body, spearing slowly through his tissue; he is lost, all hope of survival has departed from him. As I watch unseen, he falls back to be caught in the outstretched arms of his throne, he reaches for his knife, slicing into the cloth, cutting it from his wrist, not knowing that it is to late. Seeing for the first time the black swelling now revealed, a dark canker growing out of a slight wound, a death waiting to be claimed.

Panic broke out, the Dwarf, Knight and Elf surge forward, each reaching pointlessly for their weapons, determined to protect their King. There is no protection available for the disease that pulses through him, robbing him of his royal state. Denigrating him to be the long fated and the first chosen one, who much like me, must forever fight the evil presences on this world. The King is dying, and all will be well, all will be as it has been prophesied so long ago.

I left the future returning to my own time, thence to my cavern home. Sealing the entry so that the rider who must fail, will fail, once he arrives; I enter into my cavern home. All is as I have left it, except that Nailstead has expounded his total armoury of magic and curses against the walls of his prison, and like the rider to come, he too has failed. His head slowly raises at my arrival, drawn from his slump in the place set aside for the defeated, a dark place that he has been visiting. His thoughts returned, but without the darkness that previously encapsulated them. I let him speak.

He talks of sorrow and death, of life and destiny, of the future and the darkness, he speaks in ways and languages long forgotten or never learnt, he speaks clearly, but mostly he has for now accepted his fate. It has become clear to him and he accepts it, now he is reaching out with his words which were intended only to sooth my ears and calm my concerns and my thoughts at what I must do, at what he must do.

We will I know meet again and again, then he will have altered at each meeting through the coming time, then he will be my mortal enemy, but now he reaches out to me as a friend as a companion in magic, as a part of a growing legend. The dark one would forever remain in my memory as being a friend, one that I must always fight and defeat, but still a friend. The time has come for him, my lost friend to be cast down. It falls to me to thrust him from this world, and start once more the progression of fate.

The crystal cage lifts from the floor, carried by my will alone, out and away, carried to join the last residue of his minions, destined, as are they, to enter into the abyss. He floats, contained in my cage of power into the cavern of the abyss, as behind him the last rays of daylight fade, an illumination of the abyss killed by the walls closing behind him. Held captive by my energy, which like the light of day failed to reach into the abyss, turning fragile as the last vestiges of freedom faded, an energy that now could not contain him, could no longer stand against the weakest thrust of Nailstead. It shattered at his first touch, freeing him into the murky world of the dark abyss that for centuries untold will become his prison.

The light disappears, the magic has been completed, the abyss is now closed, Nailstead is banished from my world, and so for a time peace will return, but then there are the prophecies, and they all tell of his returning. Of battles to come, of heroes and heroines of life and death and of the final prophesy, which to me is swirled in murk and mystery. I have my role to play, my duty to comply with, and I Vindec have accepted my fate.
