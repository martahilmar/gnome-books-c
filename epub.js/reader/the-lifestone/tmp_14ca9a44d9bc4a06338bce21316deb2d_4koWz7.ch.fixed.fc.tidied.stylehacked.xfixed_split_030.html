Chapter 24

The King stood on a low wall, looking out over the plain, tracing the black road with his eyes. He realised the magnitude of their intent, the road ran for miles stretching out all the way to the distant foot hills. It commenced at the tree and just went on, an arrow of dark leading to an abyss. Thirty-feet wide & black as the heart of a pirate, and all of it had to be shattered, dug out and moved, according to Vindec and his plan.

A thousand willing volunteers lined either side of the first five-hundred feet of the road. All understanding that under no circumstances could they enter onto the track way, for the black covering had been infected with evil, an evil which still resided in the road surface. The dark one had used this road and his residue remained latent but potent, eager to destroy any foolish enough to enter onto it.

The King looked out, five-hundred feet distant from where he stood, twin rocks, massive rugged rocks, sit sentinel like to either side of the road. Thirty-feet separated them from each other. He knew what they had been and now he understood what they would become. Wagons lined up nose to tail behind the men, all empty, all low backed with low sides, all pulled by the finest, strongest, pairs of horse or beast available. Men held their picks and shovels ready, just waiting for the instruction to begin to calve away at the road.

Vindec's plan called for the lead men to shattered the surface, then those behind gather every trace of the black and loaded it on to the wagons, which then carry the material away. Children formed a circle from the sentinels outwards, waiting to place the material along a flag demarked narrow band.

The King had questioned the choice of children to do the work of laying out, but Vindec explained that only the most innocent could be allowed to place the black material. Only they would be safe to touch any of the fragments of the road with their hands. Every other person having contact with the material will have to do so only with their shovels or face their own death. The roadway is the source of darkness in this world and whilst it remains so the darkness remains. The King had no option but accept the truth of the magicians words, but he made arrangements for more children, and enough food and water for all. He insisted that no child work more than one hour before being replaced.

The day would pass in an endless chain of children stepping up, emptying a wagon and filling every tiny gap of the selected land with the black stuff taken from the dark road, before going back to their classes and play, with the promise of the greatest feast day ever held ringing in their tiny ears. A feast for all they were told, once the road was removed and the first stage of the plan was completed.

He, the King raised his fist into the air, he took a deep breath and at the top of his voice he bellowed out a challenge and an instruction.

"Let the dark road fade from memory, destroy the evil amidst us!" He lowered his arm as the first of the metal tipped pickaxes cut deep into the surface of the black road. Within the first hour, wagons loaded to maximum turned away from the road, carting away their first of many loads. Children eager to be involved reached out for the contents once the wagons arrived and began the long job of scattering the small slabs of black compressed road, inches deep across the narrow band of land that Vindec had carefully marked out with flags and poles. They were creating a barrier, one which.

"I would only expect to stop ants at best!" Said the King to Iffilditch as together they watched the children scattering the small black chunks! Vindec heard the Kings concerns and leaving a captain of works to carry out further instructions, he walked over to the confused men.

"That will!" He insisted "That will, would, stop all that should not enter from entering!"

Neither the King nor Iffilditch could understand how, but with no option, they put their combined trust in the magician; each for different reasons, but both gave their support whole heartedly, there was little other choice for them. Fedic looked on from a position ten-feet further away, he was not so sure that the magician was all that he claimed to be, but as a wise leader and an experienced politician, he deferred to his King and co-councilor. Accepting gracefully that he was out voted in every decision by the two, but he would wait and see the outcome of so much activity expended...

By the end of the first week of work a system had been developed. During the morning the pick men hammered and cut away at the road, the wagons hauled piles of black material and deposited it at selected places by either side of the markers. Then as the heat of the day faded columns of children, lead by their teachers, march up to the piles, and knowing that they were needed, they worked with a will to lay the chunks evenly between the flags.

From a distance, and high up, the road could be seen to be vanishing, all trace of it removed, whilst a circle of black began to form. Two arms reached out, one to the left, the other to the right, stretching out to form a perfect circle. One day soon that circle would join and the road would be gone. Twin mounds piled high, lined the entry to the inner circle leaving only a single gap. That gap would be left clear for a time, then once all was completed within the circle only then would the last of the black material be placed, closing the circle for eternity.

A month of laboring passed, then another, until finally the road was gone, the circle bar the gap complete. The King who had ridden with Iffilditch down from the Elvin castle, sat astride his milk white mare watching the figure of Vindec walking the inner circle. Vindec walked, dragging a stick in the dry soil behind him, whilst being followed by two oxen that were pulling a harrow, turning over the soil to make a shallow trench.

Later that day men gathered together summoned by Vindec, each armed not with weapons, but with spades. They listened, they failed to understand the reasons for doing what they were instructed, but accepted the instructions never the less. A column formed up, each man in it shouldering his spade, and marching through the thirty-feet wide entry gap into the circle.

Vindec placed a single flag at the center of the circle and here the men began to dig. They marked out a square then cleared the land; they dug down to the height of a man's shoulder, then moved forward, narrowing the square. They dug again. The soil lifted from within the square was carried by wagon to the inner edge of the circle of black and piled high. The second square deepened to shoulder height and the men closed again, and again, digging deeper into the earth of the plain. They dug down thirty-five feet in this tier forming manner, halting as instructed at a square, twenty-feet on a side, and thirty-five feet below the upper rim of the land.

Vindec the following morning selected ten men from the diggers; he picked the strongest generally, but also he picked men that all had the ability to keep secrets. Though their silence was guaranteed by other methods, it made it easier to ensure by the selection of certain males. The rest of the men set too, scattering the piled earth over the surface of the black circle. The men were warned not to step on the black, once covered the surface would be safe for them to stand on, but uncovered it was still a real danger to them.

All went well for many weeks, until a forgetful human made the mistake of allowing the tip of his toe to touch the black. Instantly his foot burned, he screamed in agony, and only the swift action of Vindec and a surgeon he had commanded to be ever present and watchful saved the man from a horrible death. Though it was necessary that he lost his leg to save his life! By the time it was removed, the leg looked charred and had formed pustules in which black maggots grew. The man served as a constant reminder to the other diggers, and there were no more accidents, nor did any doubt the word of Vindec from then on, they had all seen graphic evidence of the truth of his words and the warnings he had issued to one and all.

The ten men gathered in the lowest tier of the square, Vindec stood before them holding a flag pole that he had placed in the ground to indicate the final and deepest point of the structure. The men and one dwarf listened, their feet shuffling in anticipation, there was a feeling amongst them that they had been especially chosen, and the excitement they all felt, though contained, was palpable in the air.

Vindec explained what he expected of them; they nodded as one and without further instruction began digging out the final deep pit, with its soon to be created cavern. The hole they were to construct looked like a yard long flagon in shape, long, straight and then forming a bulb on the far end but with a flat floor, located at its deepest point, they cut out the cavern! The men had dug wells, created water traps and knew their business, they set too, and soon the ground changed as a hole-ten feet across delved deep into the land. Vindec watched, satisfied that they understood and then he departed, climbing the multitude of ladders that had been placed to make access in and out of the pit easier.

Builders waited for him on the surface. Masons carrying their stone shaping tools, laborers selected for their strong backs. They crowded around the magician, eager to be set to work. Wagons kicked up dust as they approached from the far side of the Elvin castle; stone had been cut into manageable blocks, then removed and shipped to this plain from the distant quarry. Block after block loaded onto wagons arrived. They would feed the laborers first, who then in turn would feed the masons, they who were charged with constructing Vindec's vision. The stone was gathered shaped and made ready prior to the work of construction commencing.

Normally when building a house you start with the foundation, if there is one and work upwards. Vindec insisted that the masons instead build a long curving wall, twenty-feet tall and sixty-feet long; as an after thought he had them extend the curve length another twelve-feet, bringing it to seventy-two feet of blank wall. He then insisted that every inch of the surface be polished smooth, and then left! No decoration, no images carved, nothing to define it but its presence, and the shadow it cast throughout the heat of the day.

Next he ordered pathways and decorative arches and then a low wall to surround the area; bit by bit Vindec constructed the grounds of a palace, one fit for a King and a home for magic. None of the masons understood his reasoning, and at one time or another, many of them questioned him, but always he just smiled at them, and after listening to their concerns, he then instructed that they continue.

Had it not been for the presence of the King and the advice of Iffilditch there might have been a bigger argument, but Iffilditch used his political skills to pacify the masons concerns, and slowly a castle of sorts began to take shape, built in the wrong order, but built just the same. The structure appeared to the senior mason to be a rambling affair, with no thought to its function. It had a large meeting hall directly beyond the main entry, followed on by huge, well equipped kitchen, which extended the length of the structure still more, making the building appear long and narrow. Room's jutted left and right from the main, its high ceilings gave it more the feel of a church, rather than a castle to him whenever he studied the edifice.

The roof was a work of art, it had more timbers, pillars and reinforcement plates than any building of its size could possibly ever require. The walls were five-feet thick, and just to make the job harder, Vindec had insisted that it curve around the huge hole in the ground, still being dug out by another team of men.

What really bothered the team leader was not the complexity or the strangeness of Vindec's design, it was a simple thing, he looked at the structure for the thousandth time and still it bothered him. This castle, for want of a better word to describe it. This castle would accommodate a large group of people; it could swallow up bodies with ease and with space to spare, in all respects, but one! It was the one missing aspect that brought him face to face with the magician, his King and the King's advisors.

"Sire I am troubled, I think that there is a mistake in the design!"

Vindec smiled, so the man had finally decided to raise his concerns, he had seen the troubled look on this master masons face many times, but each time the man had shaken his head and returned to work, having kept his concerns to himself. Now he was standing before them all, his head slightly bowed, trying to have corrected that which he believed to be wrong. Feeling, Vindec was sure, slightly overwhelmed by the people to whom he was talking, and particularly by the powerful magician about whom he in essence was stating, had made a mistake!

"Continue!" Vindec did little to help him, this matter had to rise sometime, he just hoped that it would die as fast as it rose, or a part of his plan would be revealed and it was to early for such, deep thought must at all costs be misdirected away from the only real truth.

"Sire!" He glanced up before continuing. "Sire the building does not work!"

"How so good mason?" Asked the King glancing towards the magician and seeing a slight smile etched onto his face.

"It can house so many people Sire, it has the capacity to feed hundreds out of its massive kitchens, it could even host a massive ball if required, but what it can not do Sire, is provide for the most basic need of all guests. Sire, there are no sleeping quarters, not one bedroom exists on the plan, let alone the number such a building could reasonably be expected to require!" He fell silent, his duty as he saw it done; now it was up to them.

"This building will never be slept in, no living person will reside within the building so there is no need to provide for rest places!" Vindec explained, hoping that the man and more important to him, he hoped that the King and his advisors would accept the simple statement. In its own way Vindec was telling the truth, he always told the truth, it was a part of his makeup to never lie, but the truth can be told in segments, and in this case, that was the truth of the matter, segments only.

The King looked at his mason, then at the building with its single floor and massive apex roof, then he turned to face Vindec, joining Iffilditch and 'Fedic in studying the magician. The mason departed, dismissed by a simple wave from the magician.

"Is he right, have you overlooked something?" Vindec smiled, before replying.

"No Sire all is exactly as it needs to be."

The King listened, then the memory of that voice floated into his thoughts, 'trust this magician' the voice had demanded or was that reassured he wondered, but either way he should act as bidden and trust in Vindec.

"Then I will trust to your judgment and we shall see what we shall see." Vindec nodded, his misdirection had done exactly what was required of it.
