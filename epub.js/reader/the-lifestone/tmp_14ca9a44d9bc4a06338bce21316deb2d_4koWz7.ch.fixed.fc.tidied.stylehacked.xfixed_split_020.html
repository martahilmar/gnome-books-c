Chapter 15

My troops formed up ready, others arrived from behind summoned from their hiding place by my officers, many appeared to both the right and left of me. We were ready, our numbers less than the force we faced, but trenches were dug and traps prepared. Now is the time for me to fall back and join my men, I to need to be ready to repel our mounting enemy. I turn once more looking towards the mountain home of the witch. Wondering if she would let us down, the same thought had flitted through my mind a hundred times, each with no answer in response, would she?

I lead Iffilditch across the rough ground towards the black road and back to where it had all started for me. I intended to command from the very end of the black road, in the place that I had first been discovered as a boy, long years ago to my memory. More troops appeared from hiding all around us, taking up battle places along a natural ridge, hiding behind stone walls, spears poking forwards, butts driven into the earth to resist any charge; each preparing himself to face the rage of the battle to come. Hundreds, no a thousand, no more of them, all waiting, all fighting their own fears as out there, Nailstead's army swirled and expanded slowly building its strength. Each eager to over throw our resistance and claim the spoils of war from my people.

The dwarf had been right, we had to contain them on the open ground, we must destroy them where they stand, no matter how many creatures face us. If we can effectively contain them out there, then the remaining forces will become stacked up behind them, and they would be trapped ineffectually in the valley beyond. Over there I knew, and my troops knew, are thousands of desperate foes, all eager to join with us in battle, desperate to crush us, and all that stands before them, is us. In comparison to them we number only a few, but a dedicated few, driven by the need to survive, to save our lives from total annihilation. I salute my troops, receiving a cheer of good will and faith in me back from them. All is ready, except for the witch! I have seen nothing other than the first, the opening thrust of weather by her magic. Now like every soul defending the plain, I have to place my trust in her and the power of the stone that she wields.

The tree I had studied as a child stood alone and proud, reaching out to encompass our world, I imagined it as being the last barrier, its arms stretched out from a withering thorn encrusted trunk, defying the enemy access into our lands by its presence.

The closer I walked to the point of no return, the stranger my head began to feel, my headache seemed to be back and with a vengeance, my thoughts clouded over, tearing me away from the battle, back to somewhere I did not want to go. I stumbled, clumsily tripping over something unseen, Strong Elvin arms encircled my waist pulling me upright again, I half turned trying to smile, feeling as though I was falling again. With the help of those arms I was vaguely aware of being sat down, a voice talking to me through the fog of my thoughts, I tried to answer it, but the just fog gusted thicker, damping out the words, removing any conscious understanding of their meaning. A black void opened in my mind and I fell through. Around me, unknown by me, the battle commenced.

 

As the fog cleared, I was standing looking along a dark road, my feet inches from entering onto it. A voice, a kindly familiar voice spoke to me.

"That my son is the road to your destiny, you must walk it alone, be brave and all will be as it should be." I turned, facing the midriff of a woman, looking up, I smiled into the face of my beautiful mother, she has always been with me and now she is sending me away, confusion exploded in my thoughts, what have I done wrong? I screamed out mentally. Why must I leave, what awaits me along this dark black road?

"Child I am not sending you away, I am sending you to complete the prophases; your time is fast approaching."

The lines scared into her face filled me with tears, my mother looked at me, she with single tear forming in her eye, on one level I knew that she was not my real mother! I knew that she had found me or so her tale went, but she had acted as my mother, and I thought of her kindness and care of me as being motherly, but now she wanted something of me, something scary and desperate. She needed me to leave her, to be brave and face my future. I did not feel ready to face the future, but choice and readiness' had no bearing on the here and now of her wishes.

She shimmered infront of me, vanishing as I had seen her do before. She has power in her old bones, a power that I as a child do not understand, but I have witnessed used many times. When asked by me about her life, she always answered that she is a white witch, a good witch, provided by the fates to give aid to me. Now as she vanishes, leaving me facing the long road, I wonder.

Slowly as I step out along the road, a cloud confuses my thoughts, my memories of our time together fade until I am a lone child, a little boy, standing now at the end of a long walk, exhausted by my efforts, staring at a tree growing out of a stone plinth. Her face is gone, all memory of her removed, and I am alone, I am an enigma only to the soldier that would soon discovered me.

My life has travelled the route that she expected of me, yet I had not known that, nor had I recognised her delicate features or aged wisdom when she had tested me for the council. Now I sat looking out at the battlefield watching as my troops clashed with Nailstead's, listening to the air rent with the screams of death, not knowing that my mother or the woman that had acted as a mother to me, was also the witch in the mountains that I, and all civilization were depending on for our ultimate salvation.

 

"Sire are you all right?"

 

Iffilditch was tending to me; he had loosened my tunic, removed my breast plate, and was trying to reach my mind with his questions. I tried to nod, I tried to speak to him, to reassure him that I was fine, but all I could do was cough. A hacking, chest ripping cough, which finally cleared the fog from my lungs, allowing me to breathe freely once more. The clean air replaced the fog, which in turn cleared my head returning to me my thoughts, but with the memory of my encounter with the witch remaining. I knew her now, I still had no option but to trust her, she would shine through just as she had said she would, it was not the people or the coming battle that she was manipulating, it was I, and it always had been I realised at long last.

Far from where I sat realising how fate had been usurped by the witch, the dwarf was looking out over the left flank of my defences, thinking hard.

 

His friend the King was over there waiting for the battle to explode in violence; the first waves of Nailstead's army had been contained after a fashion, out there on the plain. What have we missed wondered the very puzzled dwarf knowing that there was something, so little just a hint of some concealed meaning to an event unseen or misunderstood, something important perhaps even deadly to their cause. Desperately he sought for anything that might clarify the threat he felt looming ever closer to them all.

He thought back to an interview with the King held a few days after his safe return from his scouting mission. He played that interview through his mind. He was in the King's private office, sitting next to Iffilditch the Elf, talking about the rock trolls. Iffilditch had described his encounter and the amazing flight of such large creatures. Unfortunately, no sighting or sign of the six formidable enemies had so far been reported. They had just flown of into the night sky and vanished.

The three of them had discussed the incident but with no idea as to what they, the troll's, were about, or where they had gone, there was little other than being watchful that they could do. Still the dwarf puzzled over their disappearance, he came to the conclusion that they were hidden somewhere just waiting to break out and cause mayhem on an unprecedented scale in the only way that rock trolls could.

He also realised that six rock troll's would be very difficult to halt. Now he was sitting feeling concerned looking towards the battlefield, but as well as watching he was also wondering how might they, the rock trolls be used by Nailstead, and it was only when he asked himself, 'who they might be targeted against,' that a mental picture of the creatures rearing up from within the earth, and charging towards the King filled his mind.

Before he was even aware of it he had jumped to his feet and charged towards a horse mounted lancer, reaching up he grasping the horses bridle, he demanded that the rider dismount. The Lancer just looked down at the frantic dwarf not recognizing him, and remained sitting firm in his saddle. He was not going to give up his horse so that a dwarf could run away from the coming battle. With contempt in his voice he said.

"Get back to your position you ..." That was as far as he got! It is only a fool that calls a dwarf a coward, and a fool does not deserve to be treated with respect. The dwarf reached out, flipping the rider's foot out of his stirrup, and with a sharp tug he unseated the lancer, who fell, tumbling in a cacophony of metal armour entangling to the floor. The dwarf had the rains in one hand and was pulling himself into the lancer saddle with the other, whilst geeing the horse up and away. Dwarves as a whole are not good riders, and he was no different to others, but at least he could stay in the saddle reasonably well.

All around him voices shouted for him to halt, some lancers obviously friends of the unseated rider, turned, clearly intending to give chase to the dwarf, but under bellowed orders of their officer, they about faced once more.

"Let the dwarf go" shouted a sergeant of the guard who had recognised the horse stealer, and realised that something important was happening.

"Face the enemy, be ready," the sergeant bellowed. "That is the King's Dwarf and he must be about something special!" Having disentangled himself and reordered and replaced his armour, the fallen lancer looked about him for another horse. All about him his friends were now laughing, which did little for the fallen's temper; he swore all manner of evil revenges that he would extract on the dwarves body when next they next met. He found a spare horse, not such a good one as his that had been stolen from beneath him, but sound enough. He mounted, returning to his unit with a last baleful glare after the fast departing dwarf.

The dwarf pushed the strange mount as fast as the creature could run, soon it would have to be slowed to a trot but the dwarf intended to cover as much ground as he could, as fast as possible. He raced past the lines of defenders riding mostly out of control, desperate to attend to the King's side. He knew where or at least who, the rock trolls were going to target, and his loyalty to his friend and King demanded that he attempt to carry him a warning. He could only hope that he would arrive in time to save the King from a certain death, even if doing so meant his own demise. Only the King's safety held importance for the dwarf as he swerved the horse around an arms wagon, spooking its team by the closeness of his rounding. The wagon driver raised his whip but only shook his fist at the fast diminishing dwarf before continuing with his necessary journey.

 

Far behind the battle lines, at the base of a rock cliff the ground began to shake, something moved the loose earth, digging its way out of the ground. Soil clods flew threw the air, scattering and shattering into dust, as powerful fingers flexed upwards. The Rock trolls had arisen.

They had flown across the nighttime skyline unseen. Their landing had been far from subtle each had crashed into the rock cliffs further below the entry point of the witches cavern than was intended. There they had used their massive strength to carve out first a hiding place in the rock. Then they had begun in earnest to dig. For a hundred feet they dug straight forwards into the cliff edge until they as one stopped, slowly they turned their attention upwards.

They dug and scraped out rock and soil tunneling towards their goal. Near to the surface they felt the heat of the day warming the ground which they dug through. Break-out would occur soon if they continued, so they again stopped their digging, instead sleeping away the next few days in anticipation of the attack to come.

They would know the time to launch themselves out of the ground, to batter down the solid door and force entry into the witches cave system. They were clear of intent, there was only one thing that they had been charged to do, 'kill the witch!' The chant repeated in each of the resting creatures brains, 'Kill the Witch,' time after time, nothing else mattered, no one else mattered to them, just the single desire, deeply imprinted into their thoughts mattered to them, 'kill the witch, dead!' Crush her out of existence and be sure that she is dead! Stomp her body into the ground, be sure to kill the witch, dead!

 

Nailstead stood on the high ground which overlooked the distant spread out plain, his attention fixed on the closed in weather beyond him, knowing the source of his troop's misery he looked out across to the distant mountains. His instructions had been given, his battle plan instigated, and his most powerful allies were right now preparing their attack. Soon the witch would be dead. The King would have lost his most powerful of allies, and from that point on, his regaining the power that had been usurped from him would be assured.

The King, the usurper would soon be dead! Beneath the dark cowl that always covered his head, the dark one smiled an evil smile one of pure pleasure to come at the mayhem about to be unleashed.

His troops died before him frozen where they fell; but he had plenty more of them behind him. Those out there were the distraction, the link to him that served only one purpose, to occupy the King by dieing. Witch created snows billowed as he raised his arms, time for him to distract the witch, time to fight back. He sent a wind billowing out from his fingertips, a wind that would swirl around the falling snows, gathering them together before finally blowing her storm away. His troops would be revealed to the King's archers, they were already gathering together, as more poured along the black road then spread out seemingly as the front spur of his attack. Below him thousands would die but by the end of this day, Nailstead was certain that he will have won his victory, and the open unresisting lands beyond the King's army would be ripe for his picking.

"Those that have betrayed me will suffer my wrath, their families will suffer, and their friends will suffer, everyone must suffer for my pain." Nalistead could feel his mounting excitement, he screamed out to the billowing winds, gusts born of his own creation.

'They must all suffer! I will rule this land as I should have, and any that oppose me will be crushed into dust his thought raged.' "No," he screamed! "Not dust, less than dust, into nothing, nothing, do you hear me, nothing!" his words were born away from him on the wind.
