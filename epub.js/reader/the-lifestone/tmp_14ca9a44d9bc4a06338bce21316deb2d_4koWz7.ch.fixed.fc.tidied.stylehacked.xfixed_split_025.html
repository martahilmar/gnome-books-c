Chapter 20

Whilst the King spoke to the nearest of his men, on either outer edge of his defensive box, troops closed in as Nailstead's army a force depleted departed before them, leaving the field of battle for another destiny. Slowly the area cleared and as the last creature slithered out of sight, the ground once more stood barren and empty, bar the littering of shattered corpses and the blood stained patterns woven across the heavily churned up grass.

Many creatures had frozen where they stood, having perished in the witches storm of ice, wasted lives lost, just to please their dark lord! Leaving behind a scattering of flesh made figures, puppets without strings, which would finally collapse to the ground and be gathered eventually for the fire pits, all bodies would be burnt to cinder to defend against infection spread.

Already an army of flying scavengers were appearing, black dots swelled from within dark flitting clouds, then they settled out of sight, coating the corpses, increasing the decay by their hunger. Silence fell across the battlefield, the silence of the dead, the silence of a defeat.

Near to the King a crowd stood listening, eager to show their support of him that had led them to this point. Soldiers felt the elation of being alive, of having survived the confrontation unscathed. Others amongst them felt fear which surged up, set free now from within their thoughts. Battle fatigue flowed, though for most present there had been no battle. Still the feelings exploded and the King stood infront of them watching, slowly being surrounded by a mass of men, which swelled in steady ranks as more realised the truth of the situation. Life was destined to return to normal, they could go back to their homes and hearths, hug their families, pick up their children and be proud that they had faced both their fear, and the enemy with honour.

 

Whilst all this was going on Vindec faced the crisis that developed in his cavern, not the witches cavern home he told himself, growing in stature, his cavern home, and it was invaded. A single entity stood before him, it glanced around, seeking something other than him. To his left the stone rested pulsing blue silver light into the room, aware of the dark lord's arrival; preparing for the upcoming battle.

Inside of the stone the linked mind of the witch reached out towards the statue still form of Vindec, only to be rebuffed by him. Her aid was not needed! Only Vindec would face the dark lord, only Vindec could defeat him. The stone should do its job, it should create and fulfill the prophesies, but it would be only Vindec, no longer the water carrier boy that must defend and defeat this dark entity. The enemy of all that was right, stood before him, waiting, accessing, looking on in confusion, he had not expected Vindec, he had come to destroy the witch.

 

Nailstead was no longer the human boy that Vindec knew he had been once. He was no longer human in the true sense of the word. The dark forces had clasped him to their bosom, molding the helpless boy's nature to fulfill its own desires. They had creating from the boy king a creature of pure evil, a monster; they had formed and guided the dark one. He that was destined to rule and destroy, a creature that represented all of the dark magic that had ever been driven from the light of the world, and he is filled with the desire to provide a place for their return.

Nailstead does not know that nothing of the person he had once been remained, or that his mind, will and body have been totally suborned to the will of the darkness he now served. He believed that he was still the rightful King! That he would and should indeed rule, that his status as lord would be returned to him and thus he believed that he was destined rule, just as he wanted to. What he does not know is that the power that controls him, is indeed ancient, it is a force of witch magic, long ago turned from the path of light and led by confusion. Once drawn in he was led further by his greed and hatred, along another darker less visible path, one that desired not a ruler of people, but the destruction of any that oppose it.

Nailstead knew nothing of the long ancient prophesy, or of the book of power which guided the ancient darkness to seek a return to its former glory. Nothing is told of the battles, the deaths, the long black night of a thousand years. Nothing at all is told to the dark lord, only the stone knows the truth and now the witch, but neither will speak of it less it become a reality.

The opponents of what both considered to be the last battle faced each other, in a room of ancient power lit by a stone far older than even the thoughts of man, Elf, Dwarf, and dark lord all combined. A power nearly as old as the planet on which they stand. A stone formed in the fires before land, a stone discovered by the elves of old, in the closing time of darkness, but a stone that precedes all of the magic that had formed, good or evil. The stone waits, prepared to do what it must to protect not the people of this world, but the planet of its birth.

The voice that cut through the air carrying with it all the acquired venom of a pit of vipers.

 

"Where is she?" It demands of I, Vindec as I stand my ground before the cowled figure of the dark lord.

"Where is she?" It screamed again, the sound reverberating of the caverns walls. To my left the stone glowed brightening the room, casting out the dark one's shadow. His attention flickered from me to the stone. He begins to understand I divine. I prepare, knowing that soon it will begin; this creature of the other world will launch its final act of treachery, and without any warning. In the vain hope that it can eradicate my resistance to it in one single stroke, it began to speak.

"Where is..." The lightning struck, flaring across the room, aimed as I had expected at my heart. I moved slightly, twisting my body, turning sideways, extending my hand, flicking out a finger. Force met force, struggled together for a second or two then died back to their respective hosts. The dark one looked shocked; his cowl had tilted slightly back with the returning jolt of his emanation. He realised, now he knew the truth of my power, of the threat to him I posed

"Who are you?"

The question seems pointless to me, what did it matter to him who I was, only that I stand between him and his desires and hold a danger and power equal or greater than his own.

 

"Leave slug of a man or die horribly!" He challenges.

 

A nice try; I respond not with a lightning thrusts, but with laughter. The stone pulses, I could feel my heart beating in my chest, the stone could also feel my life essence, it matches the dull pounding thud, filling me with a deeper understanding. Her, the witches voice echoes through my thoughts, demanding control from me, wanting to face the dark one. I resist her demand, knowing that if I once allow her to utilise my body she would take control of it fully, and then it will be her, the witch, that resides in this male form, not I.

She intends to, desires to, have it all! The power of the stone by her blending of her mind and also the power over me taken by stealth. Nailstead, the dark one is mine to defeat. The stone will aid me but as for the witch within, I am not sure that I can trust anything she offers to me as assistance. I have exceeded the knowledge that she has gifted to me, and now I am standing in this cavern as the epitome of wizardry. Nailstead seems to still be searching the room, his dull lifeless eyes scanned and sought, sifting through every crevice. He half turns away from me, almost ignoring my presence, seemingly with contempt. Then before I can even see his slight turn back, he is accelerating around, twisting, spinning his form, his cowl whipping out as gravity tugs against the material. The battle is commenced, he now knows who his enemy is, and has engaged in the final battle, the battle for total control.

His attack fills the air with red streaks of light, which pulse out a multitude of lightening, some aimed at me, others passing harmlessly to either side of my body. The he lunges, a single light ball of blood red glowing power thrust towards me. I realised my mistake, the harmless seeming tendrils are not harmless, they restrained my body to one spot, held ready to be decimated by the real power seeking my destruction, held tight and compressed within the red ball, which is now hurtling towards me. Move screams a female voice in my head, I ignore it, instead, calmly, almost infinitesimally I lift my hands my palms rotate fingers uppermost.

Blue light swims before me now, a shield of energy, just as the deeper voice of the stone instructed. All around me light floods forwards forming into first an oblong, then swiftly expanding upwards and outwards, creating a barrier to Nailstead's lunge. They collided, light flickering, flaming and finally the struggle is lost, it explodes, hurtling energy back towards the creature that attacked me.

That energy smashes into his cowl, pressing against it hitting back hard, flattening the cowl against the thin emaciated body of the dark one hidden beneath it, and outlining him with his garb. Depicting to me very clearly, the cost that his chosen union with the ancient forces of the night, and its evil, have born as a toll upon his once stocky frame. Nailstead resists his own energies assault, but then my shield extends, forming into a cage, one with a fast closing once open door.

The creature that had once been the boy King Nailstead is now battling for his life and sanity, he is vulnerable, the Lifestone knows it, I know it, and now as the cage encircles his distracted body, Nailstead knows it; he has been defeated and so the penalty for his actions awaits him. Glory and power are gone, snatched from before him and by a mere human. His anger flaring in his breast succeeded in only channeling more defeated energy into his fading spirit.

The cage door snapped shut in a glittering display of electric blue light. Nailstead the Dark Lord is held captive, the threat he posed has been removed, contained, subjected; as soon his physical presence will have gone. He like his true minions will be cast into the never place, to wait and exist through the passing of time. He will reside in the nothing of nowhere forever, if the spells needed to bind him can be created.

I understand the power that is needed, I understand the duty and sacrifice I and others must voluntarily make, if we are to ensure that the abyss is protection enough against the dark one. I know that despite the restraints, he will attempt, and perhaps succeed in an escape. If allowed he will become the nemesis of this world once more. It is my duty to ensure that should this happen, should he escape once more, that the power exists to defeat him, and return him to his captive state. All this I instantly understand because the stone of power decreed that I understand.

The witch voice no longer permeates the thoughts of the Lifestone, I can sense her presence faintly but it is only as a part of the whole, not as an entity in its own right. She no longer exists; she has fulfilled her destiny and created a living entity within the ancient rock. She will reside with the Lifestone forever, always in readiness for the dark ones return. Waiting patiently to assist those in the future that are selected, tested and become the chosen one by the ancient, yet to be formed magic. Those destined to resume the on going fight of good verses evil will be able to utalise its strengths and direct its energies, but even that may not be enough to contain such as he forever. One distant day he will return un-challenged to reign, it is prophesied in the legends of the world and then he will rule, for now though and far into the future he is contained.

She, the witch is now a watcher and waiter, whilst others wield the real power. I wonder if she is happy with her new existence. It seems to me that she did not intend for her presence to fade into the Lifestone, but she intended to gain the power of it, and I realise that she also intended to gain the power over me in her plan. She failed, the dark one failed, but now I must not fail! I have a lot to do and quickly, but in the passing of time, quickly is not a word that has any real meaning. The Lifestone will contain what it has created; it has the power alone to succeed to do so for generations to come, at least! So, swiftly is a state of desire with my own mind alone, the abyss awaits, the Lifestone awaits, all for me to act; it is nearly time for the dark one to join his minions.

A thought, a concern burst into my mind, whether it was of my own making or sent out to me by the faintly glowing Lifestone. I didn't know, but whichever, it needs acting on immediately.
