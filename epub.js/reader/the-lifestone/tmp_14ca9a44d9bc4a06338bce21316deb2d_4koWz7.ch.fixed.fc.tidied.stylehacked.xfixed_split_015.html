Chapter 10

"Sire, a demanding hand shook my shoulder bringing me out of my memories. Iffilditch stood beside me shaking my shoulder; behind me raged the battle, apparently in its last throes. The encircled forces of Nailstead were slowly being whittled down, my troops had closed in, tightening their encirclement; but something was wrong. Some tiny detail made little sense.

"Sire look!"

I looked, not really seeing anything at first, but gradually by looking through the air still cluttered with arrows, I saw a sight that struck me to the core.

From out of the mountains, marching in neat order there came three lines of beasts. Pennants flying, each held high on long spears. Nailstead had managed to commit further forces to the battle. The widest phalanx of his minions marched along the black road directly towards me. My airborne archers turned their attention to this column, but their attack was disturbed by the sudden and unexpected arrival of a dark cloud of creatures. The air above our heads was rent by the screech of madness, as the living cloud engaged, tearing into my forces with an animal ferocity. The twist and turns of warfare have reared their heads, Nailstead had indeed been more cunning than I had expected.

Still, like all good General's I over estimated my enemy, preparing for the worst, but hoping for the best. The time had come; I ordered a signal sent to the witch. I had no doubt now in my mind that Nailstead had committed his reserve forces, I just had to hope it was done with a feeling of desperation, not with cold clear planning or intent.

My response to his invasion was now totally out of my hands. The final battle was set and the players in position. My time to plot was now over; I have become superfluous to the situation and its outcome. I had plotted and planned to this point. Luck had been with me so far, now I had to trust that it would remain so; a feeling of frustration flowed deep in my veins. My sword pommel fitted into my hand as I withdrew it from its scabbard, I was ready.

Iffilditch stood beside me facing towards the enemy, waiting for the battle to come. If the witch failed, if her conjuration did not contain our surging enemy, then the sword I hold tightly in my grasp, would for the first time be coated with the blood of my enemies. Something moved behind us, I half turn looking between myself and Iffilditch.

A massive creature approached. It's shaggy head swinging, as huge paws indented the earth. It stood half as high as myself, despite walking on four pads. It was a wolf, for a second my heart leapt into my throat, then I realised what I was looking at. It was the wolf, Iffilditch's wolf. I watched as the distance between it and us shortened, behind the creature I saw an archer grasping for an arrow. I waved my hand towards him, indicating that he stop. I double checked that he had, his hand still hovered near to his quiver but not reaching in, I could see his grip tighten on his weapon but he obeyed my signal, much as I was sure he did not wish to.

Iffilditch became aware of the approaching creature, his hand dropped to his side fingers extending. The wolf sidled up to him, its shaggy head lifting until Iffilditch's fingers contacted his furry head. The wolf opened its mouth showing for the first time the long shiny white fangs that made his kind so dangerous. I trusted Iffilditch's judgment and for some very strange reason I sensed that the wolf meant us no harm.

Turning away, ignoring the disconcerting presence; I faced forwards again, readying my mind, seeking peace within, prior to the expectation of imminent violence. Iffilditch seemed relaxed, gently fussing the creature that has adopted him, waiting like myself for a development that might have us all fighting for our lives, or might bring this battle to a final end.

 

My thoughts wandered back to that room just a few (but long) days ago, Iffilditch sitting patiently before me describing his sole invasion of our enemies lands. His scout had been successful as was defined by his presence before me. I listened as he began to talk, continuing the report of his scout, providing me with the latest, most accurate of assessments of our waiting enemies.

 

"The wolf led me out of the valley twisting through the woodlands and heading deeper into the unknown. I didn't feel threatened by possible ambush not with the creature before me and scenting continually. Any creature unfortunate enough to be hiding before us would not have the benefit of surprising the wolf, and therefore me either."

"The wolf led me deep into the enemies lair, along narrow tracks, through dense woods; never slowing, always sure of where it was going. I followed, not because I had to, but because for some reason I understood that the creature was taking me somewhere, somewhere important, to see something relevant. We circled a mountain, sleeping rough with no fire for comfort. We were too deep into enemy territory to give our position away with a fire. Anything could be watching alert for any sign of intruders. The wolf slept next to me, always a reassuring presence. For Two days he led and I followed, together rounding the mountain, then he turned towards it, climbing upwards and around still further."

"Cresting a final hump of rock, rounding a large pillar of stone, the valley below me lay unfurled in the early morning mists. Tent after tent stretched as far as I could see, creatures of all shapes and sizes materialized through the mists. An army the like of which I have never dreamed of, was camped below us just waiting for the order to rise up and swarm into our lands."

The wolf flopped down ignoring the sight that had taken my breath away. My first instinct was to retreat, to return here and pass on my observations. I would have done so instantly had I not sighted a body held secured to a stake, and had I not recognised who it was. Sire, the body as I am sure that you know, was your dwarf! I am no fool Sire."

 

I interrupted Iffilditch sharply. "I know that, I trust you beyond all others, but you must understand that there are many in the chambers that do not know you as well as I do, old friend. I know that you feel betrayed, but you were not! There was no way that I could know that you would return safely, so what choice did I have? I sent three of my most trusted out into Nailstead's lands, knowing the dangers they might face and the fate they might also face if captured, but having no other option but send them, leaving me to pray that all three would return safely."

 

"Saving your friend from the hostiles seemed to be paramount, obviously I reasoned that he would not be here if you had not ordered it so, therefore I had a choice to make, it was near thing for the dwarf, because my second instinct was to leave him to his fate, and return with the information. I still believe that doing so would have been the correct thing to do. Instead Sire I let my feelings for you sway my common sense, I decided to make a rescue attempt but without risking capture, so no brave, death defying invasions of the enemies camp for me, just doing something that would give the dwarf below, something of a fighting chance, if I could!"

"The wolf was sitting next to me, totally ignoring the field of canvas spread out below; it seemed content to lay there forever if necessary. Once I decided to move, the creature rose to its feet beside me, ready to fight and die I believed, if the need became necessary."

"Exactly how I was going to assist your dwarf to escape, at that moment eluded me, but assist him I would. I led the wolf further around the mountain finding a narrow ledge which allowed access on to an outcrop which overlooked the camp perfectly. Below and about four hundred yards away was the bound dwarf. The creatures that had brought him in, and had been gathered around him when I had first seen his plight had departed, mostly. I thought that I could put an arrow into both guards, even considering the distance, and I had nearly settled on doing just that, when a slight disturbance drew my attention beyond the dwarf, towards an evil looking tent.

Either side of the tent were two creatures, both looking exactly like the ones I had hunted and killed previously. They stood upright, at attention, looking very proud, but not overly alert. I think they felt safe standing outside of that tent, in and amidst the hubbub of their encampment; little did they know how easily their lives could be snatched away from them. Perhaps I wondered, killing one of them would cause such a disturbance that the dwarf could affect his escape. I looked back at the bound dwarf, just then and realised that he was already in the process of doing just that, of escaping!

"I watched as he sliced his wrist bonds apart. But still he remained in place by holding tight to his supporting bar. I could see his arm muscles were now tensed; he was apparently not quite ready to disappear. Though how he might hope to escape through that camp I hadn't a clue, but obviously he had some plan. I pulled an arrow from my quiver fitting it to my bow and took aim at the left hand guard, his death would cause mayhem to break loose and thus offer the dwarf his much needed distraction."

"Just as I was about to loose my shot, the tent flaps parted and two creatures walked into the light. One dark, tall and black cloaked, Nailstead himself I realised, hastily I changed my target. To his side, not really drawing my attention walked a tall gangling, sort of human looking creature. My arrow flew straight and direct towards the Lord Nailstead's chest, with luck I remember thinking Sire; I have perhaps just ended the threat to our peoples. Alas, at the last second my arrow altered course, why I am not sure, but I think Nailstead somehow detected it and diverted it. It missed him completely, piercing instead into the creature standing next to him. Nailstead looked up at my hiding place, waved his cowled hand in my direction and then retired back into his tent again, closing the flaps."

"I looked down, the dwarf was gone, vanished Sire, just disappeared in a puff of smoke! Where to, I did not know, nor did I understand then how, but he was gone; just as I should be. Arrows were splattering against the surrounding rocks, soon one or two would hit me or the crouching wolf. We retreated, vanishing from their sight, seeking the protection of the mountain and the anonymity created by hiding. The wolf paced ahead as I hurried behind, we made good time, covering the ground in a wild exciting fleeing."

"Creatures were clearly audible, screaming in anger and struggling up the cliff faces behind us, the wolf charged on, I followed, trusting to his survival instincts. From infront but at a distance a screeching erupted, high in the air I heard yet another distinctive sound, it was the sound of fast approaching wing beats. We were cornered, trapped by land and air, both forward and back. The wolf without hesitation swerved upwards, thrusting into and through the light bushes; I followed, feeling the terror of imminent capture flooding my actions, using that fear to thrust myself forwards after the wolf."

"He entered a cave at a dead run Sire, turning in a skidding tail swishing stop that defied the eye. I followed thinking that this was as good a place to die as any. Below us the screeching sounds joined. Above, but now out of sight, mighty beasts wheeled across the land. Pirouetting above the dry dirt, driving loose particles into the air with their wing beats. We waited, I behind the wolf; behind me the cave descended, I looked back not eager to delve into that darkness, but I was forced by the closeness of my enemies and the backing up of the wolf to enter into that pitch.

The dark swamped me; the tunnel twisted slightly cutting off the last vestiges of light. The wolf halted before me, crouching low, growling deeply, letting all know of his presence. Something crossed the cave entrance, blacking out the light, the wolf lunged forward, his deep growl turning into a raging snarl. I pulled my bow from my shoulders, but hesitated as the wolf halted his lunge."

"The creature searching out there had obviously seen the wolf, knew the beast for what it was and had ignored it. The cave with its snarling occupant struck off from the list of places to be searched. It was then that I realised that the wolf had not been visible to those in the encampment, they obviously thought him to be a wild animal, alone and occupying this cave system! Therefore I reasoned that they would reason, that whilst the wolf was inside of the cave, no man or elf would dare to enter, so the assailant they were hunting had to be elsewhere. The wolf by its cunning and understanding had provided me with a true safe sanctuary; the creatures outside of our lair would hunt the lands, searching for me, never knowing that they had been within feet of me."

"I sat down, finding an outcrop of stone, the wolf seemed to understand, it returned as nature would dictate to a wild animal, into the dark. Leaning against my bent knee, more pet or friend, wanting my attention. I rubbed at its large pointing ears, grateful again to the wild creature that had saved my life, repeatedly, and was fast becoming a much trusted and even more cared for companion."

"The night passed, continually pierced by the night shattering screeches of failure from outside, the wolf lay at my feet as I curled up against my pack, which was placed against the cave wall. I kept my bow next to me but trusted more to the keen senses of my companion to alert me to any incursion of our sanctuary. As for the dwarf Sire, at that point I could do little but offer my best wishes for his safety and escape. We together had combined as a single unit l and wolf, we had done all we could that day, I slept feeling quite pleased with the outcome of the venture so far. Now I had to return to you as a matter of priority to carry my warning to you of our enemies' strength and intent."
