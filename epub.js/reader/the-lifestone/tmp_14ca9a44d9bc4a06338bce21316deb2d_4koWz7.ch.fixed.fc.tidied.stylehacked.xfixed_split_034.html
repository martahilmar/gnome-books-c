Chapter 28

King Arthur, lord and master of all that stood for good, for peace and for life, stood now before them all. He looked to young to some of the witnesses to bare the title and its awesome responsibility. He stepped forward bringing him closer to the gathered. He reached out with a smile to Vindec, who as was right had stepped to the side of the room, he alone knew the news that the King intended to impart. Iffilditch looked puzzled, almost frightened, not by the meeting but by the sure feeling that he detected within his King.

As he listened to the King speak, Vindec found his admiration grow for the man. He with a clear controlled voice, spoke of the evil of the battle fought, he told all there of the Olar, the changeling marsh, created to protect the people and the world. He told of a future where life would have changed so much that perhaps no one presently listening would be able to recognise their home or world any longer.

He told them of his plan, he told them of his call to arms, of the ninety-nine volunteers he would be choosing, of the one hundred milk white stallions that would be required for the long and dangerous battles ahead. He told them all that they needed to know, all that Vindec had explained to him earlier and in private that very morning. He did not tell them that he the King of King's was dieing! That soon, if he remained as King his body would rot away, as the poison within his blood had begun to spread once more. This he did not tell them, because this was not how he wanted to be remembered, he was and always would be the King of Kings and those who rode with him, rode into legend and folk law.

He called for volunteers; he demanded only the bravest most true of his knights come to him, from which he would make his selection. He called for horses to be provided, Milk white and pure bred, the finest steeds in the land. Then to everyone's surprise, he asked the people to pray for him and the selected. He asked them to talk of their King, waiting through time with his finest knights, always ready to Sallie out into the world, to defend this his realm against any and all that would destroy it.

As he spoke, he did not know that the legend he was creating of a brave King in waiting would spread, passing through time and civilizations, living forever until the final Day of Judgment. This even Vindec did not know, but it would happen, destiny had ordained it.

Having spoken for an hour without a break he dismissed the room, bidding all to spread the news, to inform the uninformed, and to gather all that he had requested, for in one months time, as the sun rose to light the world. One month from today, at the time of the seeding celebrations, when people naturally gathered to bless the crop planting, he would call fourth the volunteers, and select his chose ninety-nine knights.

As the door opened to allow the excited listeners out of the council chambers, word began to travel, guards who had been listening told others that had not heard. People rushed out talking amongst themselves, spreading the King's words; already the legend was forming, taking root and being spread to the world.

The room emptied until just the King, Vindec, Iffilditch and Fedic along with the original scribe remained. The doors started to close when a male slipped into the room, guards reached out whilst verbally challenging him, but the dwarf ignored them all. One guard got to close and felt the light graze of powerful animal teeth brush against his skin, the wolf accompanied the dwarf and at a signal from the King the guards allowed him through, closing the doors behind him adding two more to the remaining gathering.

Arthur stepped forward, reaching out to curl his arms around the taciturn dwarf, pleased that he had arrived back at the city. He had been sent to assist and supervise the destruction of the enemies corpses. He had also been charged with ensuring the security of the hills and valleys surrounding the castle. Now he was back and the King was pleased to have his most trusted friends around him.

The door banged as a gloved fist knocked, and then the door opened just enough to allow the head and one shoulder of the King's guard to appear around it.

"Sire, another dwarf to see you!" The guard uttered.

"Show him in," replied Vindec, stepping forward to take control of the situation. The guard waited, ignoring the magician until the King replied.

"Show him in please."

The guard stepped back allowing the door to open further. A dwarf entered, having to turn side on to slip through the narrow gap, though most present thought that he was probably as wide in all directions so turning was rather pointless, still he entered and the door once more closed, this time with a finality to it which all felt.

Everyone in the room including the lowly scribe recognised the dwarf standing before them. His girth was legend, his love for fine food and real dwarfish ale preceded his person, but mostly he was known for his skill with metal and the creation of durability combined with function and strength, and even in some cases deadly beauty.

He is a dwarf of considerable age, the son of an elderly weapons maker, who like him was the son of a weapons maker. Both longevity and skill run in his family, and he is also the same dwarf that Vindec had visited with earlier that morning. He stood in the room looking at his leaders, and wondering? He had heard the fast growing legend and he knew his skill and therefore had a fine idea of why he had been summoned. What exactly he was to construct he did not know yet, but he soon would, so a little patience would carry him a long way.

Vindec crossed the room towards the scribe sitting at the only table in the room. The scribe watched, suddenly realising that he was in the magician way, hastily he cleared the table-top of his paraphernalia and stood aside, watching. Vindec removed a leather tube hanging from a thick leather strapping, which rested across his shoulders. He opened the buckle which sealed closed the end cap, and slipped a single parchment out from within. A bag made of course cloth, small of size but clearly solid of content, thudded onto the table top. Vindec ignore it for now.

At a wave of his fingers he unfurled and flattened the parchment displaying for all to see twin diagrams of a sword, a very intricate sword, a sword with power glowing from even its drawings. The dwarf examined the images, touching the parchment, feeling the blade residing within, whilst knowing that it fell to him to construct this fine weapon. He looked at it in detail, noted the length, breadth, shape and design, then he nodded more to himself than to anyone present, he glanced up at the magician.

He pointed towards the fighting guard, Vindec reached out to pick up the bag he had so casually allowed to fall; he untied its top and spilled out four tiny ruby red stones and a slim sliver of rock with an unnatural glow to it. Iffilditch heard the sharp intake of breath from the King standing beside him; he studied the sliver then he looked up at the magician, and understood. This was the stone shard that Vindec had placed in the King's hand so may days before. Now it rested on this table for all to see its inner life glowing.

The King looked pale to his friend, but that was nothing as the realisation of what the slivers presence meant. Iffilditch understood that the King, his King, was once more dying. The sliver was no longer protecting him from the poison, so the King is dieing! He tried to reach out to take the sliver intending to place it once more in the King's hand, but something stopped him. A voice echoed in his mind.

"None but the chosen may touch any part of my body!"

The voice commanded with such direct force that even with all his will exerted, Iffilditch could not make his fingers extend to pick up the sliver, even to save his friend. The King touched him; a feeling of inner peace flooded through the clearly distraught Elf. The King understood, now Iffilditch also understood the call to duty had been sounded the prophesies had to be enacted...

Vindec placed the five stones back into the bag and handed them to the stunned dwarf. He nodded, dumbstruck, realising the challenge. The parchment rolled up tight and Vindec placed it safely back inside of the tube, this he handed to the dwarf craftsman. Saying.

"None may be told of what you undertake; never may you describe the weapon you are constructing, not now, not ever! Your life and the lives of your future generations will be in grave danger if any outside of your direct lineage are ever to learn of the dwarf who made the key to the future!"

Again the dwarf nodded. Guards entered the chambers forming up beside and around the craftsman called by a silent thought sent out by the magician.

"Twenty and nine days!" The dwarf looked up at the magician's words, then he nodded for a final time before he departed, with his personal escort, his royal bodyguards. Leaving the chamber to work his own brand of magic in the dwarf foundries were he lived and worked, with a commission the like of none that he had ever undertaken, and so little time to complete it. Twenty and nine days, a task worthy of his talents, a challenge to be faced and met.
