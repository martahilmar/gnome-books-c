Chapter 31

With the last of the guardians passed and the Olar now behind them, Vindec halted the donkey. Before him a crowd of eager children surged forward, cheers arose from the adults behind them, as the children raced towards the Olar. Each child took from one pile or the other, a piece of black rock. Carrying it tenderly then placing it into the gap left open as an entryway; slowly the gap was filled, sacks of shaved wood were scattered across the black until it had been covered fully. Adults stepped forward, each armed with a shovel, why they had shovels Vindec could not say, but they had! Each adult dug a spade of clean earth from one or other of the piles and as the children closed the gap, they shoveled the earth over the black, covering it completely.

Soon the circle of the Olar was completed; from that day fourth no normal person could tread the ground, only death awaited the unwary within. Guests were actively resisted by the protective entity of the Lifestone residing within.

Vindec told the crowd of the threat they faced if they tried to enter, they understood his words, accepting them, and that day another legend was set in place. The Olar had been ordained a place of death and none would live to challenge it should they try to enter. As the magician talked to the gathering the ground heaved and from it sprouted the last piece of the Olar's defense, the gap filled with bush and thorn sealing it forever.

The granite reared mountain like, above his head, Vindec strained his neck looking up, trying to see the place that he had called home. The tunnel into the rock heart that led up to the cavern had melded away. Now only a bleak wall of solid granite greeted a visitor. High above his head the cavern that had once looked out across the lands was no more, that portal was vanished, melted and sealed into the stone of the mountain.

Inside the cavern still existed, Vindec knew that, but now there was no way that he could ever enter it again. Vindec would need to locate a new home, one in which he could reside as the centuries passed. He turned away and departed from that place.

Within the Olar, within the cavern of light beneath the castle, basked the sleeping figures of ninety-nine knights all dressed in full battle armour and one King, all chosen and waiting for the call to arms. The sickness within the King's blood progress not at all, it like their bodies was frozen. Though when they awake the poison will also awaken and the King will commence dying again; until he returned to rest once more.

The Lifestone's pulses protected its charges, whilst it also secured the walls and gates of the abyss. It would protect all for many years to come, but in time there would be challenges, as those held captive within the abyss fight and think of ways to escape. When that finally happens, all will not necessarily be lost, for safely in the Lifestone's care, are the knights and the one true King!
