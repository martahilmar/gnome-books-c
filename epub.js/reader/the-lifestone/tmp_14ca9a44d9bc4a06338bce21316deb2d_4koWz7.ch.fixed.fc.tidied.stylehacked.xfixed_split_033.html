Chapter 27

Vindec turned away from the long hall and walked from the new and final resting place of the Lifestone. All around him life grew, the ground moved beneath his feet as the stone reached out to its home, defining, altering, and creating the land within its realm. The Lifestone also reached out to Vindec, with the voice of she, which echoed in his thoughts, confirming his belief and wishing him well in his life. He passed between the twin guardians, then through the only remaining exit from the circle. He walked on a ways before turning to look back over what he had created.

Where there had been a ring of soil there now was growth. The ground swelled as root after root of snake like vines burst from within. Trees unplanted grew, filling out the inner space, within but unseen, new pathways formed each leading to somewhere, but never to be walked by him again, he understood. Vindec would be welcomed only once more into the circle until then his life would pass in waiting, in watching, in helping his King. Eventually even that duty would change, but that would be far into the future and not to be considered now, even by him.

He turned towards the distant Elvin castle, he has a meeting at midday with the council, but first he needs to plant the seeds of knowledge in the mind of the one that it will most affect. He spread his arms out, reaching with his mind for the sky, and with a single beat of his outstretched wings Vindec took off. Leaving the ground below him, bursting upwards into the air; to any that saw him he appeared to be a large eagle, soaring high into the sky, few if any saw him, but Vindec with the sight of an eagle saw all below him. Including the growth of the vines, which grew incredibly, already in just a few minutes the vines had taken control of the ground and covered it.

Dark tipped thorns were forming already, each a deadly barrier to any foolish enough to enter. The entry guarded by the sentinels remained clear of the vines; only once the last of the black road was put in place would the vines conquer that narrow gap as well. The trees grew a foot as he watched, by the time this day was complete the area would be unrecognizable and fully protected. All that remained was for the prophesy to be completed, and that was close, as close as a few wing beats, as close as a will born idea. The one he sought waited, but without knowing that he waited for the magician to arrive, unknowing that he waited for his life to be changed forever! Destiny calls on feathered wings...

 

Vindec explained, the King listened; sitting in his office chair facing the magician, fingering the sliver of stone that had so far save his life or so he had believed. Now came the news, the demand for his service, the future was clear he understood, but he found it hard to accept. Vindec looked at his King knowing that what he was demanding of this fairly young man was much to ask, but also knowing that the King had no other option other than to accept. The prophesies assured him of that, all that remained was for King Arthur to accept the duty and its challenges.

Vindec left the King; he called into visit with certain members of the elf world, then he sought out one dwarf. He was an elderly dwarf, well respected but equally temperamental, but his skill in the working of metal was incomparable. They talked, and finally Vindec departed.

 

The council chambers were full, seats previously unoccupied were filled, the council sits in all its august majesty. The King entered a look on his face of calm ordered thought. He nodded to his ministers and took his place between his twin leaders of the council. Iffilditch watched, wondering at the look of peace that emanated from the King, as he glanced at the silent, still Vindec, knowing on some level; that this meeting would not be like so many others.

Iffilditch at a nod from the King silenced the room; a feeling of expectation flooded the space, emanating from the council benches, outward through the gathered and still further. All could feel the magic in the air, all realised that this meeting had been called for a good reason. All present understood that they the representatives of all races had been gathered, not for the dealing with of mundane affairs but for a serious matter of state. They sit as bidden, in silence now, waiting; some even holding their breath, others clutching their own hands, all wondering at what they are about to hear.
