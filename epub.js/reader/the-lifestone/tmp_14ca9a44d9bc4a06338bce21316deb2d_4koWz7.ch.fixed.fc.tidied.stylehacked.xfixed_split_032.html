Chapter 26

Vindec stood alone in the dark, his only company the empty back of a flat wagon. He waited until the time arrived and then his hand reached out to touch the wood of the wagon.

In the cavern that Vindec called home, a strange light filtered and brightened in a snaking column, inches wide, but extending in its length with every passing second. The entity of the Lifestone reached out across the floor, stretching out, seeking an exact point. The room faded as the light departed, only the dull glow of the light snake lit the cavern now, but in the passage off the cavern, the light burned intensely bright. The point was selected and the light delved between and through the solid floor, altering the granite structure to allow it to pass.

Below near the front of the cavern a split occurred, one tongue pulled away from the snake light, departing outwards towards the dark night, molding and reshaping the rocks as it flowed. The original tongue carried on, worming through the granite until the rock itself changed, becoming no longer rock, but water, frozen solid water. The light entered reaching into the ice, seeking the center of the room where it expanded, filling out the cave.

Ice did not melt with the invasion, but mental resistance and control by another faded from the four entombed figures. The rock trolls felt the light enter their lives, felt the power that flooded through their minds, and accepted it as being right. Soon the ice melted, soon the rock trolls captured by a magician, sent to destroy the Lifestone and to kill the witch, by the dark one were freed, both from the ice, and the control of Nailstead.

The trolls moved, stood up, turned, and felt life again; their strength was now in demand and their service to the stone essential; for magic could not be used to move the stone! Magic cast at the Lifestone would be repelled, driven into the person delivering the magic, thus the Lifestone protects itself.

This cavern which is currently its home could only be temporary. Out there waited its real home; a place of safety for it to reside, somewhere created to and destined to protect its entity, throughout the passage of time. The Lifestone calls and all obey.

Vindec did not notice the passing of time or the relocation of both him and the wagon. He appeared at the base of the granite mountain, facing four huge powerful rock trolls. They lowered their collective heads as the air shimmered revealing him to them. Surprise, shock, even questions are not in the vocabulary of thought of the rock trolls.

Whatever happens, they accept, without question or interest. Their only real emotion is confusion, and then only when they do not have a way forward or have to make a decision. Usually they sit waiting for someone or something to decide for them, then they move, rock steady, unstoppable by any normal means. They have no understanding of where they are, or how they got there, nor do they have any interest in understanding, they have a job to do, a way to move forward and a voice to listen too.

Only two of the trolls would be needed for what must be done, the other pair would remain by the wagon, they would stay for as long as it is required of them, time had no meaning to them. The chosen pair stand, behind them Vindec can see the new entrance to his home; gone are the steps and narrow passages, which led the invited through the granite walls, replaced now by a slope, one wide enough for a rock troll to walk through. They enter, Vindec leading, they followed him up the slope, which gently sweeps left as it climbs until it enters into the cavern room. The tunnel leading to Vindec's room is gone, replaced by a solid wall, his meager belongings now sealed away for all time.

The crystal waits, he can hear it calling softly to his thoughts, demanding gently, that now is the time! The trolls hear nothing, but follow as they should. Vindec knows that it would appear to be far easier to use magic to relocate the Lifestone to its new home, but that to try to do so would be the death of him, the stone and magic can never mix. They will repel each other and destroy the user, before the Lifestone submits to any such spell; which left Vindec with only one option that of muscle and walking, coupled with carrying, as the only way to relocate the glowing power.

Though Vindec could and had touched the surface of the stone, the rock trolls could not; it would be instant death for them if they tried. A cradle rested on twin supports, created by the Lifestone in readiness for this day. Vindec approached the stone, lowered his head, communing with the entity within. She soothes his mind, assures him that all will be well, and so Vindec bends, wrapping his arms around and beneath the glowing stone, he lifts it.

He feels the weight compressing his bones, it is heavy to lift alone, but equally he knows that he can do so. He swivels, shuffling his feet around until with genuine relief he lowers the stone into its waiting cradle. His physical part in re-homing the Lifestone is now complete. The rock trolls approach, one to the front one to the rear. Massive arms, capped by dense huge fingers close around the twin carrying bars, they stand, lifting the weight as though it was nothing. To them it is as nothing, just a little something they need to carry for a while.

Lead by Vindec they depart the cavern; shortly the Lifestone and its cradle were secreted hidden from mortal sight within the back of the wagon. Two trolls stood by the back board, whilst the other pair took up the position normally occupied by the horse team. Vindec sits in the driver's seat. They move, the trolls behind leant forwards pushing the wagon on, whilst the rock troll horses pull the wagon onwards. Vindec sits in silence, watching the massive backs flex as footstep follows footstep, pace after pace. The wagon travels not especially quickly, but constant and unstoppable, towards the ring of black and the well of the Lifestone, all that he has seen created is in waiting, and soon all will be in its correct position.

The trip passed in silence with not even the sound of the wagons wheels turning, grating against their hubs filtered up to Vindec, they were seen by no one, heard by no one. Despite their passing close to settlements, through a village, and between two drunks that were verbally abusing each other across the width of the road on which they traveled.

Magic could not be used against the Lifestone but it could be cast around the presence and Vindec, sitting in silence in the driver's seat, he was holding a balloon of nothing tight to the passing vehicle. None would know of this nights work, none would suspect the power being moved through the land. Not even Nailstead a prisoner in the abyss would know of the relocation of the Lifestone at least not until it was safely ensconced in its new home.

Dawn closed on the party as it closed on the black circle. Soon they passed between twin rocks, rocks that had previously guarded the black road but which had like their companions come back to life and had during the long night moved to a new place, sitting once more, twin giant pinnacles of stone there to wait. Nothing moved as the wagon and its cargo entered into the circle, the rocks remained rocks, knowing that what passed them by was as it should be. They, the rock troll sentinels would only react to invasion by those not permitted within the circle but nothing else would stir them from their rest.

They sit forever silent, allowing time and weeds to form and vanish, sometimes coating them in green at other time dying back revealing them fully. Vindec steered the troll's inwards, deeper and towards the main structure. Once at the entry to the long hall he halted the wagon, Then he climbed down to supervise the final installation. The Lifestone had finally arrived and soon it would be sealed inside of its new home, alone for now, but soon to be accompanied through time by the chosen.

Two trolls plucked the stone from the wagon, led by Vindec they enter into the hall passing through the doors which open to greet them, and closed behind them, hiding them from any prying eyes. Darkness surrounds the three but the light of the Lifestone grew, illuminating the room in all of its austerity. Vindec stopped, he looked forward, he pointed a single finger at the floor. The room fell even more silent, the ground before him noted their entry, noted his indication, and obeyed his instruction, welcoming all to its presence.

Before them appeared an arch, beyond it a tunnel waited. Vindec led the trolls through the arch and into the tunnel then down. They followed the curving gently sloping path to a solid brick wall, there once again Vindec accompanied by a blinding flash of light from the crystal pointed at the brick barrier. An arch, the second of their journey formed, they again passed beneath its towering curves entering into the sealed room beyond, a room created by the builders who knew nothing of its use, believing it to be empty and sealed forever.

He looked across the space lit solely by the Lifestone, seeing the bricks which seal the only known entry point into this cavern. The rock trolls stepped forward placing the support they have delivered directly in the center of the cavern, then together they turn away, their duty fulfilled, they depart, soon to be followed by Vindec. Behind Vindec the archways vanish as he passed through it for the last time, walls melded together sealing the Lifestone in to its permanent resting place. From this day forwards only the selected could enter into the Lifestone's presence there to bathe for a time in its rich glow.

The last arch closed, one of the trolls had sat down, neatly placed before the concealed entry, there to remain as a last or first guardian of the Lifestone. Only dust falling to the floor disturbed by their passing, it filtered down coating the silent rock guardian, it was now the only indicator as to what had taken place. Inside silence remained, below ground hidden from sight, the Lifestone lived. It reached out and secured the abyss, holding all that should be within, captive, as it would for many years to come.

Vindec stands before the three remaining rock trolls, his eyes studying them as the sun rose lighting the inner circle. Sunlight covered the stone muscles, his magic added to the mix and the trolls changed, not instantly, more melting away, being replaced by something new, something equally as dangerous but far more capable than a rock troll. Vindec nodded to himself as three mounted knights stood before him.

"Welcome keepers of the paths!"

Of the three only one showed signs of recognition, it also is the only one of the keepers that is gifted with speech.

"You are the magician Vindec, you are here and accepted!" The keeper turned his horse and rode out to fulfill his duty, to keep forever the paths.
